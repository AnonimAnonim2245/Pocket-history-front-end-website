Pocket History

Russian invasion of Crimeea

In 2014, Russia set a precedent that will lead later to the current invasion of Crimeea. Russia needed Crimeea due to its strategic importance in the Black Sea and to stop Ukraine from extracting the gas resources in the Black Sea

Hungarian Revolution of 1956

The communist leader Imre Nagy was a communist reformer who sought to democratise the country and pushed for the Hungarian nation to leave the Warsaw pact. He lead the Hungarian Revolution, unfortunately it was crushed by the Soviets. Do you see a pattern here?

Assasination of Julius Caesar

Caesar had been recently named “dictator in perpetuity” of the Roman Republic. Ongoing tensions between Caesar and the Senate, amid fears that he also planned to claim the title of king, overthrow the Senate and rule as a tyrant, were the principal motives for his assassination. He would set a precedent for all autocrats all around the world.

Spanish Inquisition

Before the Reconquista, Spain was dominated by Caliphate of Cordoba, after it the Spanish Monarchy started to inquisition to expunge and eliminate the "heretics" from the Spanish country.

Anschluss of Austria

Hitler was from Austria and he considered the Austrian people as Germans. His ultimate desire was to unite all German people under one country, thus the slogan "Ein Reich, Ein Volk, Ein Fuhrer".

9-11 Attacks

In bin Laden's November 2002 "Letter to America", he said that al-Qaeda's motives for the attacks included Western support for attacking Muslims in Somalia, supporting Russian atrocities against Muslims in Chechnya, supporting the Indian oppression against Muslims in Kashmir, support for Israel in Lebanon, the presence of US troops in Saudi Arabia,US support of Israel, and sanctions against Iraq.
